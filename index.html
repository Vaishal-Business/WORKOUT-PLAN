Stretch marks — cause and fix

Why you get stretch marks
At 174 cm and still growing, stretch marks are caused by rapid growth spurts — skin grows slower than bones and muscle. Completely normal and very common in teenage boys. Primarily genetic and growth-related.
What actually helps
1. Vitamin E oil or pure coconut oil massaged into stretch mark areas daily. 2. 3–4 litres of water daily. 3. Zinc and vitamin C — eggs, nuts, and fruits in your diet cover this. 4. Gain weight slowly (0.5–1 kg/month max). Your revised plan is set at a slow, controlled bulk rate which naturally reduces new stretch marks.
What does NOT work
No cream fully removes existing stretch marks — they fade naturally over 1–2 years from red/purple to silver. At 16, most marks fade significantly by age 19–20 as growth stabilises.

Progression — when to move up in weight

When you can complete ALL reps of ALL sets cleanly — move up at the next session. With only 5 kg max, you will hit this ceiling in 4–6 weeks. Budget ~150–200 AED for 8 kg and 10 kg pair as soon as possible.
Track your reps every session in your phone notes. Even +1 rep per week is progressive overload and is all you need to keep growing.
When diet and sleep are consistent, a 16-year-old can gain 1–1.5 kg of lean mass per month — faster than most adults can ever achieve.
